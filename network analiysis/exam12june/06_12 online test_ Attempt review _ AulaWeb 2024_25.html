In the PageRank algorithm, what is the primary reason for introducing the concept of teleportation?

Question 28 Select one:
a.

To bias the ranking towards pages that are highly cited in smaller, disconnected components of the web graph.

b.

To allow the algorithm to converge faster to a trivial solution where all pages have equal rank regardless of their incoming links.

c.

To prevent random surfers getting trapped in cycles or dangling nodes.

This is correct. Teleportation ensures the random walk does not get stuck in parts of the web graph without outgoing links or in closed loops, allowing the algorithm to explore the entire graph and converge properly.
d.

To increase the computational efficiency of matrix multiplication by ensuring sparsity in the transition matrix.

Feedback

Correct.

Teleportation allows the random surfer to jump from the current page to any other randomly selected page with a certain probability (1-α). This mechanism solves the problems of dangling nodes (pages with no outgoing links) and cycles (sets of pages that only link to each other) in the graph, ensuring that the transition matrix is stochastic, primitive, and ergodic.

The correct answer is: To prevent random surfers getting trapped in cycles or dangling nodes.
